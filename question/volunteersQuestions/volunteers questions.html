volunteersQuestions


Who are the volunteers at FarmTime, do they have an age limit? Do they have previous experience?

Registering for Sando as a volunteer is easy and fast. All that is required is to download the application, fill out the user profile and... start volunteering! The minimum age to register for the application is 15. Teenagers between the ages of 15-18 are required to have parental consent which will be provided in the application at the time of registration. After that, every time you register for a future activity, an email will be sent to the parents. Teenagers under the age of 15 cannot register for the application by themselves, but can come to the activity accompanied by an adult Sando user (over the age of 18) who has registered for an activity that allows this, for example - a family activity.


Who are the farmers in farmtime? Is it possible to volunteer in the whole country?

The farmers in FarmTime are farm owners from all over the country who appreciate the benefits of using volunteers and are eager to provide them with a unique experience. In the personal profile of each farmer, you can learn about his farm and the activities he hosted in the past.


Can the farmer ask me to do any kind of work?

FarmTime is intended exclusively for direct activity in the fields of agriculture and grazing (sheep and cattle). For each activity opened in the application, the farmer is required to indicate exactly its type in order to allow you to choose the activity in the best way and to know exactly what is expected of you on the day of the activity. However, there may be cases in which the farmer is forced to change the planned activity (weather, last minute cancellations and more), in these cases the volunteers are expected to show patience and goodwill.


How many hours does an activity day include?

A classic volunteer day at FarmTime is around 4-5 net work hours to which must be added work briefing times and breaks. The working hours of each farmer are detailed on his activity page and if something is not clear, you can always find out more details in a chat or a phone call. By and large, any arrangement agreed upon between you and the farmer is legitimate, but note that even volunteering requires a minimum work time commitment, otherwise it is not effective and does not help the farmer. Therefore, in most cases an activity of less than 3 net working hours will be unlikely.


I would like to combine accommodation with a farmer as part of the volunteering, is this possible?

Many farmers offer accommodation options in order to improve the experience of the volunteers and start the day of volunteering on time. Each farmer who offers lodging lists in his profile the conditions (tent / trailer / room, toilet, shower, etc.) and what he has to offer in addition (area for lighting a fire, kitchenette, cooking utensils, etc.). However, in order to ensure a good experience for all parties, we recommend verifying the details in a conversation with the farmer before the activity.


I have never worked in agriculture, how can I know what activity is suitable for me?

On each activity page in the application, the hours of activity, the nature of the work and the level of physical difficulty are detailed in order to help you as much as possible to choose an appropriate activity. Also, if something is still unclear, you can always contact the farmer via chat in the app or on the phone and ask additional questions.


What is important to bring from home for an activity day?

Any activity in FarmTime requires long pants and closed shoes. In the hot seasons, a hat, a shirt with long sleeves and water are also recommended (the farmer always takes care of water in the work area, but it doesn't hurt to bring). In addition, you must bring food and lodging equipment (if required) as agreed with the farmer


What is expected of me (the volunteer) on a working day?

After matching expectations and confirming the details with the farmer there shouldn't be many surprises. What remains now is to arrive on time with appropriate clothing, listen carefully to the training before work and make sure to work according to the safety rules. It is important to remember that the work that is done is the farmer's livelihood and must be treated with respect.
